The Ramadan



What is it ?

Ramadan , sometimes spelled ramadhan or ramaḍān ( Arabic : رمضان ? ) 1 , is the ninth month of the Hijri calendar 2 , 3 . The only month whose name appears in the Koran 4 , 5 , Ramadan is for Muslims the "holy month par excellence" 6 because it is the month of fasting (or Saum ) and contains Laylat al-Qadr (the night of Destiny) 7 . In French as in English , the word “ramadan” is used interchangeably to designate the holy month for Muslims and, by metonymy , fasting or saoum 8 . Fasting in the month of Ramadan is one of the five pillars of Islam . During this month, Muslims, that is to say adults and children who have reached puberty according to the currents of Islam, must not eat , drink or have sexual intercourse from dawn to dusk . sun 2 , 9 . Ramadan is considered the “month of charity” because, when it ends, the faithful must pay alms, zakât al-fitr 10 . It is also the month in which many other significant events in Islamic history are commemorated .6 .

What breaks the fast?

1. The sexual relationship
Fasting is first of all abstaining from eating, drinking and having sexual intercourse during the day. Therefore, when the faster commits sexual intercourse, his fast is canceled. He must necessarily proceed to a so-called expiatory repentance (Al – Kaffârah) and which consists of: the freeing of a slave (believer), if he does not find a slave, he must fast two (02) months consecutively, if he cannot fast consecutively for two months, that is to say that he has a valid impediment according to the legislation, then he must feed sixty (60) poor people, with a quantity of two (02) Moud (approximately 1/2 kg) for each, of the food that people prefer to consume the most in the country.

2. Ejaculation
If this ejaculation is provoked by hugs, sensational touches, looks of carnal desire, masturbation, ... or other act depending on the will of the faster; then his fast is cancelled. This person will nevertheless have to continue his fast and repair this wrong on an equivalent day, after Ramadan. However, if this ejaculation takes place during sleep, the fasting person has nothing to fear because his fast remains valid; especially since it would have happened independently of his will. In this case, he must do the great ablutions (complete washing of the body) to get rid of the defilement [Aj – Janâba].

3. Eating or drinking voluntarily
The faster who drinks or eats voluntarily, his fast is cancelled. He must also proceed to the so-called expiatory repentance (Al – Kaffârah) as in the case of sexual intercourse. However, whoever eats or drinks by forgetfulness, his fast remains valid, as it is said in the following Hadith: " Whoever fasts, then eats or drinks by forgetfulness, let him finish his fast, for it is Allah who fed and watered ” [Hadith recognized unanimously authentic.]. However, Imam Malick advises to resume such a fast, out of caution. Beyond eating or drinking, any solid, liquid or gas introduced into the stomach, VOLUNTARILY, invalidates the fast. Also the fact of taking nutrients intravenously, carrying out blood transfusion to the fasting person or blood dialysis, invalidates his fast because it constitutes food for him. But the non-food injection does not cancel the fast, even if it is advised against by some scholars.
